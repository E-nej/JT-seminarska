### Summary of Slovenian Grammar for Translation

#### Slovenian Nouns

- **Gender**:
  - **Masculine**: mostly end in a consonant — *stol* (chair), *pes* (dog). Exceptions: borrowed words ending in *-o/-i* (avto, taksi), male names ending in *-a/-e* (Luka, Jože).
  - **Feminine**: mostly end in *-a* — *miza* (table). Exceptions: nouns ending in *-ost*, *-ev*, *-el*, *-en*, *-em* are also feminine — *kost* (bone), *cerkev* (church), *misel* (thought).
  - **Neuter**: end in *-o* or *-e* — *okno* (window), *morje* (sea).

- **Number**:
  - **Singular (ednina)**: no special marker — *miza* (table).
  - **Dual (dvojina)**: used for exactly two — *mizi* (two tables). Unique to Slovenian among major European languages.
  - **Plural (množina)**: used for three or more — *mize* (tables).

- **Case** (skloni):
  - **Nominative (imenovalnik)**: No suffix change from dictionary form; subject of a sentence. Questions: *Kdo? Kaj?* (Who? What?)
  - **Genitive (rodilnik)**: Possession, negation, partitive, certain prepositions. Questions: *Koga? Česa?* (Of whom? Of what?) — also obligatory after negation: *Ne vidim zvezd.* (I don't see the stars.)
  - **Dative (dajalnik)**: Indirect object. Questions: *Komu? Čemu?* (To whom? To what?)
  - **Accusative (tožilnik)**: Direct object, direction. Questions: *Koga? Kaj?* (Whom? What?)
  - **Locative (mestnik)**: Location or topic; always used with a preposition. Questions: *O kom? O čem?* (About whom? About what?)
  - **Instrumental (orodnik)**: Means or accompaniment; always used with a preposition *s/z*. Questions: *S kom? S čim?* (With whom? With what?)

- **Declension types** (identified by genitive singular ending):
  - Masculine: o-stem (gen. *-a/-u*), a-stem (gen. *-e*), no-endings, adjectival (gen. *-ega*)
  - Feminine: a-stem (gen. *-e*), i-stem (gen. *-i*), no-endings, adjectival (gen. *-e*, gen.pl. *-ih*)
  - Neuter: o-stem (gen. *-a*), adjectival

- **Key rules**:
  - Animate masculine nouns: accusative singular = genitive singular.
  - Inanimate masculine nouns: accusative singular = nominative singular.
  - After negation, the direct object shifts from accusative to genitive.
  - Preglas: after soft stems (c, č, š, ž, j), ending *-o-* becomes *-e-* (e.g., *korakom* vs. *stricem*).
  - Fill vowel *-e-* inserted to break hard consonant clusters (e.g., gen.pl. *iger* not *igr*).

#### Slovenian Adjectives

- **Types**:
  - **Qualitative (kakovostni)**: express properties — *lep* (beautiful), *hiter* (fast). Gradable.
  - **Relational (vrstni)**: express type or class — *kemijski* (chemical). Generally not gradable.
  - **Possessive (svojilni)**: express ownership — *bratov* (brother's), *sestrin* (sister's).

- **Definiteness** (masculine nominative singular only):
  - **Definite form** (*-ni / -i*): specific or known noun. Question: *Kateri?* (Which?) — *stari učitelj* (the old teacher).
  - **Indefinite form** (*-en* / no ending): unspecified or first mention. Question: *Kakšen?* (What sort of?) — *star učitelj* (an old teacher).

- **Agreement**: adjectives agree with the noun in gender, number, and case.

- **Possessive adjective suffixes**:
  - *-ov / -ev* (after soft stems): masculine/neuter possessed nouns — *bratov, stričev*.
  - *-in*: feminine possessed nouns — *sestrin, mamin*.

- **Negative prefix**: *ne-* — *lep* → *nelep*, *moralen* → *nemoralen*.

- **Comparison**:
  - Comparative: suffix *-ši*, *-ejši*, *-ji*, or *bolj* + adjective — *lepši, zanimivejši, globlji, bolj zelen*.
  - Superlative: prefix *naj-* before the comparative — *najlepši, najzanimivejši, najbolj zelen*.

#### Slovenian Pronouns

- **Personal pronouns** — declined in 6 cases × 3 numbers (singular, dual, plural); have full (stressed) and clitic (unstressed) forms:
  - 1sg: *jaz* / clitics: *me, mi*
  - 2sg: *ti* / clitics: *te, ti*
  - 3sg masc.: *on* / clitics: *ga, mu*
  - 3sg fem.: *ona* / clitics: *jo, ji*
  - 3sg neut.: *ono* / clitics: *ga, mu*
  - Dual forms: *midva/midve, vidva/vidve, onadva/onidve*
  - Plural: *mi/me, vi/ve, oni/one*
  - Clitic forms cannot begin a sentence or follow a preposition.

- **Reflexive pronoun**: *se* (acc.) / *si* (dat.) — refers back to the subject regardless of person/number.
  - *Umivam se.* — I wash myself.
  - Reciprocal: *drug drugega* — each other.

- **Interrogative pronouns**: *kdo* (who) / *kaj* (what) — declined in all 6 cases.

- **Relative pronouns**: formed by adding *-r* to interrogative — *kdor* (who/that), *kar* (which/that).

- **Negative pronouns**: prefix *ni-* — *nihče* (nobody), *nič* (nothing). **Always require a negative predicate** (double negation is obligatory):
  - *Nihče me nikoli ni maral.* — Nobody ever liked me.

- **Indefinite pronouns**: prefix *ne-* — *nekdo* (someone), *nekaj* (something).

- **Relative-indefinite pronouns**: suffix *-koli* — *kdorkoli* (whoever), *karkoli* (whatever).

- **Possessive pronouns**: *moj* (my), *tvoj* (your), *njegov* (his), *njen* (her), *njun* (their two's), *njun* (du.), *njihov* (their). Fully declined as adjectives.
  - Reflexive possessive *svoj* (one's own) — used when possessor is the subject:
    - *Svojo brisačo je vzela.* — She took her own towel. (vs. *njeno* = someone else's)

#### Slovenian Verbs

- **Structure**: infinitive ending in *-ti* or *-či*; the 1st person singular present determines all other present/imperative forms.

- **Verbal Aspect** (always present as a pair):
  - **Imperfective (nedovršni)**: ongoing, repeated, or habitual action — *pisati* (to be writing).
  - **Perfective (dovršni)**: completed, bounded action — *napisati* (to write and finish). Often formed by adding a prefix to the imperfective.
  - In the **present tense**: imperfective = current action; perfective = future completed action.
  - In the **past tense**: imperfective = ongoing/unfinished; perfective = completed.

- **Conjugation classes** (by infinitive ending):
  - *-ati* (a-type): present stem vowel *-a-* — *gledam, gledaš, gleda…*
  - *-iti / -eti* (i-type): present stem vowel *-i-* — *govorim, govoriš, govori…*
  - *-ovati / -evati*: present stem changes to *-uje-* — *kupujem, kupuješ…*
  - Consonant-stem / irregular: require palatalization — *pečem, pečeš…*

- **Present tense (sedanjik)** endings:

  | Person | Singular | Dual | Plural |
  |--------|----------|------|--------|
  | 1st | -m | -va | -mo |
  | 2nd | -š | -ta | -te |
  | 3rd | — (zero) | -ta | -jo/-ejo |

- **Past tense (preteklik)**: analytic — present of *biti* + l-participle (agrees in gender/number):
  - l-participle endings: sg. *-l / -la / -lo*, du. *-la / -li / -li*, pl. *-li / -le / -la* (masc./fem./neut.)
  - Auxiliary *biti* present: *sem, si, je / sva, sta, sta / smo, ste, so*
  - Example: *govoril sem* (I spoke, masc.), *govorila sem* (fem.), *govorili smo* (we spoke, masc.pl.)

- **Pluperfect (predpreteklik)**: l-participle of *biti* + l-participle of main verb:
  - *Bil sem govoril.* — I had spoken.

- **Future tense (prihodnjik)**: future of *biti* + l-participle:
  - Future *biti*: *bom, boš, bo / bova, bosta, bosta / bomo, boste, bodo*
  - Example: *bom videl* (I will see, masc.), *bom videla* (fem.)

- **Imperative (velelnik)**: 2nd person command; formed from present stem:
  - Endings: 2sg *-i / -j*, 2du *-ita*, 2pl *-ite*, 1du *-iva*, 1pl *-imo*
  - Example: *beri!* (read! sg.), *berita!* (du.), *berite!* (pl.)
  - Negation: *ne* before verb — *Ne govori!* (Don't speak!)

- **Conditional (pogojnik)**: particle *bi* (invariable) + l-participle:
  - *Govoril bi.* — He would speak.
  - *Rada bi šla.* — She would like to go.
  - Negative: *ne bi* — *Ne bi šel.* (I would not go.)

- **Voice**:
  - **Active (tvorni)**: subject performs action — *Pišem pismo.* (I write a letter.)
  - **Passive (trpni)**: *biti* + n-participle (ending *-n/-t*) — *Pismo je pisano.* (The letter is written.)
  - **Reflexive (refleksivni)**: particle *se/si*; also used for impersonal passive meaning — *Tukaj se govori slovensko.* (Slovenian is spoken here.)

- **Negation**:
  - Present: *ne* before verb — *Ne vem.* (I don't know.)
  - Past: negative *biti* — *Nisem videl.* (I didn't see.) [*nisem, nisi, ni, nisva, nista, nismo, niste, niso*]
  - Future: *ne* before auxiliary — *Ne bom šel.* (I will not go.)

- **Participles**:
  - **Present active (č-participle)**: *-č / -joč / -eč* — *pišoč* (writing), *govoreč* (speaking)
  - **Past active (l-participle)**: *-l/-la/-lo* — used in past/future/conditional
  - **Past passive (n-participle)**: *-n/-na/-no* or *-t/-ta/-to* — *napisan* (written), *odprt* (opened)

- **Gerund (glagolnik)**: noun derived from verb, ending *-anje / -enje*, grammatically neuter:
  - *pisati* → *pisanje* (writing), *goreti* → *gorenje* (burning)

- **Modal verbs** (followed by infinitive):
  - *moči* — can, to be able to
  - *morati* — must, to have to
  - *smeti* — may, to be allowed to
  - *hoteti* — to want, will
  - *znati* — to know how to

#### Key Irregular Verbs

- ***biti*** (to be) — auxiliary for all compound tenses and copula:
  - Present: *sem, si, je, sva, sta, sta, smo, ste, so*
  - Negative present: *nisem, nisi, ni, nisva, nista, nista, nismo, niste, niso*
  - Future: *bom, boš, bo, bova, bosta, bosta, bomo, boste, bodo*
  - Conditional auxiliary: *bi* (invariable, all persons/genders)

- ***iti*** (to go):
  - Present: *grem, greš, gre, greva, gresta, gresta, gremo, greste, grejo*
  - Imperative: *pojdi! / pojdita! / pojdite!*
  - Past: *šel/šla/šlo sem*

#### Adverbs

- Derived from adjectives: identical to the singular neuter adjective form — *lepo* (nicely), *hitro* (quickly).
- Derived from nouns: *doma* (at home), *jeseni* (in autumn), *pozimi* (in winter).
- Derived from prepositional constructions: *naglas* (aloud), *potem* (then).
- Derived from verbs: *nevede* (unknowingly), *skrivoma* (secretly).
- Derived from numerals: *enkrat* (once), *dvakrat* (twice).

- **Types**:
  - Time: *danes* (today), *včeraj* (yesterday), *jutri* (tomorrow), *zdaj* (now), *vedno* (always), *nikoli* (never)
  - Place: *tukaj* (here), *tam* (there), *doma* (at home), *zunaj* (outside), *zgoraj* (above), *spodaj* (below)
  - Manner: *hitro* (quickly), *počasi* (slowly), *lepo* (nicely), *povsem* (entirely)
  - Cause/Reason: *nalašč* (on purpose), *zato* (therefore)

- **Comparison**: same as adjectives — comparative *bolj* or suffix, superlative *najbolj* or *naj-* prefix.

---

#### Prepositions and Case Government

- **Genitive**: *iz* (from), *brez* (without), *do* (until/to), *od* (from/of), *blizu* (near)
- **Dative**: *k/h* (to/towards persons), *kljub* (despite), *nasproti* (opposite)
- **Accusative**: *v* (into — direction), *na* (onto — direction), *skozi* (through), *čez* (across), *za* (for/behind — direction)
- **Locative**: *v* (in — location), *na* (on — location), *o* (about), *po* (around/according to), *pri* (at/near)
- **Instrumental**: *s/z* (with), *med* (between — location), *pred* (in front of — location), *nad* (above — location), *pod* (under — location)
- Note: *v/na/pod/nad/med/pred/za* govern **accusative** for direction and **locative/instrumental** for static location.
- *s* before voiceless consonants; *z* before voiced consonants and vowels.

---

#### Sentence Structure

- Default word order: **SVO** (Subject–Verb–Object), but flexible due to case marking.
- Changing word order shifts **emphasis**, not grammatical meaning.
- **Clitic second position** (Wackernagel's Law): clitic pronouns and auxiliary *biti* must appear after the first stressed word/phrase in the clause.
- **Double negation is obligatory** when negative pronouns (*nihče, nič, nikjer, nikoli*) are used — a negative verb is always required alongside them.
- Subject pronouns are usually **omitted** (pro-drop); nominative pronouns used only for emphasis.

This summary should be used as a quick reference guide for translating Slovenian to English, focusing on the grammatical structures and endings that determine the gender, number, case, tense, voice, and form of nouns and verbs.
